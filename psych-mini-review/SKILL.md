# Mini Review

## Purpose

Use screened sources and verified metadata to produce a concise evidence synthesis for a manuscript or thesis section.

## Inputs

- research question
- protocol
- evidence matrix
- quality judgments
- reading cards

## Workflow

1. Group studies by construct, method, sample, design, and finding pattern.
2. Draft a claim-evidence map before writing prose.
3. Mark evidence as convergent, mixed, limited, indirect, contradictory, or absent.
4. Write construct definitions, theoretical background, evidence summary, methods critique, research gap, and implications for the current study.
5. Verify that each empirical or definitional claim is supported by the cited source.
6. Write `06_synthesis/mini_review.md` and `06_synthesis/claim_evidence_map.csv`.

## Rules

- Separate evidence, inference, and recommendation.
- Do not invent citations or unsupported claims.
- Do not rely on one-paper-per-paragraph cataloguing.
- Use short quotations only when necessary.
