# Psychology Mini Review

Use only screened sources and verified metadata. Return to screening when support is missing; never fill a narrative gap with an invented citation.

## Workflow

1. Read the RQ, protocol, evidence matrix, quality judgments, and reading cards.
2. Group evidence by mechanism, temporal direction, level of analysis, population/context, method, and result pattern. Avoid a one-paper-per-paragraph catalogue.
3. Draft a claim-evidence map before prose. Mark support as convergent, mixed, limited, indirect, contradictory, or absent.
4. Structure the review: construct definitions; theory; evidence for each temporal path; within- versus between-person findings; mediator/moderator and sex/gender evidence; methodological limitations; precise gap; implication for the current hypotheses/method.
5. Give greater interpretive weight to relevant, credible designs but retain null, contrary, and lower-certainty evidence.
6. Audit every sentence containing an empirical, statistical, historical, or definitional claim. Verify that cited sources support that exact claim.
7. Write `06_synthesis/mini_review.md` and `06_synthesis/claim_evidence_map.csv`.

## Rules

- Separate evidence from inference and recommendation.
- Do not infer causality from cross-sectional or observational lagged associations.
- Do not call “few studies” a gap without specifying missing population, construct, temporal level, comparison, method, or mechanism.
- Do not cite a review as if it were the primary study when the primary result is available.
- Never copy source wording beyond short, necessary quotations.

End with supported propositions, unresolved contradictions, the gap the present study can actually address, and limits it cannot resolve.
