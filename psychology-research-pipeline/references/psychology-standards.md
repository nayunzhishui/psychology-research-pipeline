## Selection hierarchy

At execution time, verify and save the current official checklist or author instructions. Apply in this order:

1. Target journal instructions and article type.
2. APA Journal Article Reporting Standards for Quantitative Research (JARS-Quant) or the applicable APA JARS module.
3. STROBE for observational longitudinal/cohort reporting.
4. PRISMA 2020 and PRISMA-S only when the deliverable is a systematic review or reports a systematic search.
5. SAGER guidance when sex and/or gender are analyzed.
6. Institutional ethics, consent, data governance, and disclosure rules.

Do not apply CONSORT to an observational panel study or claim PRISMA compliance for a narrative mini-review.

## Core reporting rules

- Define the population, setting, recruitment, waves, intervals, attrition, eligibility, consent, ethics approval, and final analytical sample.
- Name every instrument, source/version, item count, response scale, scoring, reverse scoring, time frame, interpretation, and evidence of validity for the current population.
- Report internal consistency by wave where meaningful. Prefer omega when assumptions support it; do not treat alpha alone as validity evidence.
- Describe missing-data patterns, attrition analysis, missingness assumptions, and handling method. Explain FIML or multiple imputation rather than naming it only.
- Report estimands, unstandardized and standardized estimates as appropriate, confidence intervals, exact p values except very small values, effect sizes, model fit, diagnostics, software, versions, and estimator.
- Distinguish confirmatory from exploratory analyses and record multiplicity control or an explicit rationale.
- Report all tested primary models, including null and contrary findings. Avoid interpreting model fit as proof of theory.
- Never use Harman's single-factor test as the sole common-method-bias justification.
- Make data/code/material availability and restrictions explicit. Do not promise public release of sensitive adolescent self-harm data.

## Longitudinal panel requirements

- Verify stable participant IDs, actual wave dates, intervals, and comparable measures before modeling.
- Test longitudinal measurement invariance before comparing latent constructs or structural paths: configural, metric, scalar, and only then strict where relevant. State partial-invariance decisions.
- Separate between-person and within-person questions. A CLPM does not isolate within-person dynamics; an RI-CLPM random-intercept correlation is a stable between-person association, not a temporal effect.
- Justify number of waves, lag spacing, stationarity/equality constraints, residual covariances, within-wave correlations, and time-invariant/time-varying covariates.
- With three waves, acknowledge limited power and flexibility for RI-CLPM, longitudinal mediation, random slopes, and nonstationary effects. Prefer simulation-based power or sensitivity analysis.
- Inspect outcome distributions. NSSI commonly contains many zeros and strong skew; do not default to normal maximum likelihood without evaluating transformations, robust estimators, ordinal/count/two-part alternatives, and interpretability.
- Compare plausible models only when they address the same estimand. Predefine the role of CLPM, RI-CLPM, ALT-SR, latent growth, or DSEM rather than selecting by fit alone.
- For multi-group sex/gender comparisons, establish comparable measurement and test constrained versus freed structural paths. A significant path in one group and nonsignificant path in another is not itself evidence of a group difference.
- Use temporal language such as “predicted subsequent deviation” when justified; avoid “caused,” “led to,” or “mediated” without design and assumptions that support causal interpretation.

## Self-harm and sex/gender safeguards

- Minimize exposure of item-level self-harm responses, identifiers, school codes, and small-cell combinations.
- Report referral/safety procedures when data collection involved current risk, if documented. Never invent a protocol absent from source records.
- Use “sex differences” when the recorded variable is biological sex or a binary sex item. Use “gender differences” only when gender was measured. State coding, reference group, missing/other categories, and limitations.
- Avoid stigmatizing language. Describe participants as adolescents who reported NSSI rather than defining people by behavior.

## Integrity gate

Fail the gate for fabricated references or results, unverifiable key claims, undisclosed outcome switching, methods/code mismatch, material unlogged deviations, causal overclaiming, privacy leakage, or a simulated review presented as a real journal communication.
